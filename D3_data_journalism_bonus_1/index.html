Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Income

In the chart indicated above, we take a closer look at the correlations between smoking and poverty. In particular, we are looking at the corelation between those reported to be living in poverty and those reported to smoke.

There does appear to be a correlation between the percent of those reported to smoke and the percent living in poverty. As you can see, states with higher reported poverty levels also tend to have a higher number of residents that smoke. Additionally, a third piece of information was used in constructing this chart. The bubble sizes are determined by the reported average income. The smaller the reported income, the smaller the bubble.

There are obviuous limitations to this data; however. First, we do not know what they mean when they say "smoke". Are they referring to smoking tobacco or any type of smoking? Also, does this data include the use of any tobacco products such as chewing tobacco, or they only looking at those that smoke cigarrettes. Finally, do these numbers include those that might use electronic cigarrettes. That being said, there does appear to be evidence that those states reporting a higher number of smokers also report higher number of residents living in poverty.
