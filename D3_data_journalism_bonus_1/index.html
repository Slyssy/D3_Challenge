Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Demographic Factors

In the chart indicated above, we take a closer look at the correlations between health risks and demographic factors. In particular, we are looking at the corelation between the demographic factors of those reported to be living in poverty and age and the health risk factors of the % of those who smoke and the % of those with health insurance.

By clicking on the axis labels, you can easily see the correlation between these factors. The default plot shows the relationship between the percentage of those who smoke compared to the percentage of those living in poverty. By selecting the average age, we can see the relationship between the percentage of those who smoke and those and the average age of residents living in that particular state. You can also select the Y axis labels to see the relationship between the percentage of those with insurance as compared to the percentage of those living in poverty or the average age.

So what do you think? Have you clicked on the labels? Did you see any corelations? I saw a few. For example, there definitely appears to be a relationship between the percentage of those who smoke as compared to those living in poverty. As you can see, smoking percentages are definitely higher for those states that have a higher poverty rate.
